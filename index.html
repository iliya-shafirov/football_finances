What was the purpose of this investigation?

Football is undoubtedly the most popular sport in the world. The World Cup of 2014 could act as a perfect example of such popularity. The event boasted a reach of 3.5 billion people, which is the highest of any sporting event that has ever occurred. From the financial point of view, an even more astounding fact can be observed: top 20 clubs in Europe generated a revenue of 7.9 billion dollars last season. Such an outrageous number has obviously sparked my interest and, as a result, has become a trigger for an investigation focused on the financial side of football. This article is going to look into the following topics:

  • Revenue of the top 5 leagues in Europe and concrete clubs
  • Operating expenses of concrete clubs
  • Primary sources of revenue
  • Effects of financial aspects of a team on its performance and vice versa

What are the top 3 leagues in European football and which league had the largest profit recorded last season?

The term “big three” in European football usually refers to the top three leagues in Europe: the Italian Serie A, the English Premier League and the Spanish La Liga. To begin with, it is necessary to present vital financial data about the top 5 leagues. First and foremost, it is vital to look at the most important financial statistic – the profit/loss of the organization. We can see that Premier league recoded a massive profit of 683 million dollars last season with La Liga making 400 million and Serie A surprisingly ending up in the red.

As expected, the EPL with La Liga are two biggest leagues in the world and have the largest profits. The first one possesses a global reputation of being the toughest league in the world, which leads to a higher viewership because of the unexpected results. The latter one is the competition, which unites two of the biggest clubs in the world and still hosts other great teams. Let us focus on the Serie A and try to figure out reasons behind financial struggles of the league.

Why does the Serie A struggle?

The main issue that the majority of clubs face in the Serie A is that they do not possess a renovated infrastructure(stadiums), which is going to attract tourists. This in turn causes a lack of outside investment into the clubs because without good infrastructure it becomes impossible to move mediocre clubs onto the global stage. Thus, clubs lose investment attractiveness. The reason for such an issue becomes clear, as the majority of stadiums in Italy are not owned by their respective clubs. Instead, they are a property of local councils, which fail to improve the quality of stadiums but still charge an annual payment.

What are the primary sources of revenue for domestic football competitions?

Roughly, all revenue streams can be classified into three categories: matchday revenue, broadcasting revenue and commercial revenue. Let us take a look at what every channel really stands for:

  • Matchday revenue is the number of money a league(made up by clubs) makes from ticket sales
  • Broadcasting revenue is the money that a league gains from TV stations that broadcast the competition live
  • Commercial revenue stands for money made by kit sponsorships and merchandise

How much pure revenue does every league generate?

Firstly, let us look at the overall revenue before investigating what it is made up of. We can see that the EPL again dominates other leagues as far as revenue is concerned with 4.8 billion euros generated in the season of 15/16. La Liga comes in second with 2.4 billion euros. Lastly, the Serie A comes in third with 1.9 billion euros.

How does the broadcasting revenue split differ in all three leagues?

First and foremost, we should point out some facts that can be seen from the diagram. Despite having a less overall revenue, Italian league has an almost equal amount of money coming in from broadcasting when compared to Spain. This fact can be explained by a higher domestic viewership. However, neither of these two come close to the EPL, which still is a more popular league on the global stage. Nevertheless, some measures have been taken by La Liga to close the gap:

  • All of the broadcasting rights were centralized by La Liga, so that smaller and less popular clubs receive a more substantial payment
  • The ratio between the highest and the lowest earning clubs in the Premier League was 1.61 to 1 compared to 3.8 for La Liga, but this ratio is projected to decrease over the next 3 to 5 years to around a 2.0 coefficient
  • La Liga has implemented a new system, which almost completely duplicates the one of the EPL, where 50% of the total revenue is shared among all of the teams. The other 50% is spread out according to the league positions of clubs and resource generation ability of clubs in the competition

    • Serie A uses a slightly different method compared to both leagues with 40% of the total amount equally distributed between the 20 clubs. Other 30% are spread out according to the club’s appeal in Italy and the final 30% are administered according to several factors, such as league positions in the final 5 years, league position last year and historical achievements. Even though both the Italian league and the Spanish league have recently introduced new systems, their TV broadcasting fees are still subpar compared to the EPL.

What are the trends among revenues and expenses of EPL concrete clubs?

Now our attention turns towards some concrete examples among clubs. As a sample for this investigation I have chosen 4 top English clubs: Arsenal, Manchester City, Manchester United and Liverpool. The most obvious trend that could be observed from the next two graphs is that both the revenue and operating costs of all of the clubs have increased. However, this growth was not permanent for all clubs with Manchester United dipping from 2013/14 season to 2014/15 season.

How do financial results affect performances of a team?

It is a very interesting question, as a lot of regular fans and people are interested to find out just how closely linked are the financial success of a club and the sporting success of the club. In order to find out and demonstrate the results we will do the following:

  • Calculate the Pirson’s correlation coefficient between points and squad value, points and payroll for all off the clubs in three leagues
  • Interpret the results
  • Represent all three leagues on a scater plot

However, with that comes a second question: how do you judge sporting success and financial success? Well, since we are only looking into the league success at the moment, it would be wise to measure sporting success using points gained in a league season. Also, to judge financial success of a club, it would be beneficial to use payroll of a first team squad and the squad value of a first team squad. The results demonstrate that the correlation coefficient for La Liga equals 0.791 and 0.794 accordingly, for Serie A it is 0.834 and 0.776, 0.858 and 0.93 for the EPL. It is important to point out that a higher coefficient value means a closer correlation. The coefficient value from -1(indirect correlation) to 1(full direct correlation). Thus, we can see that a higher payroll and higher squad value almost certainly relate in a better league position. It is the most certain for EPL(highest coefficient) and least certain for La Liga. Still 0.7 value is very high and certainly means that a correlation exists.

Conclusion

To conclude, this investigation has presented an overview of some basic ways football clubs function in the world. Firstly, we have investigated profits/ losses of whole leagues and looked at their revenues. Secondly, we have looked at ways clubs and leagues make money. Furthermore, we moved on to interpret all of the revenue streams. Thirdly, we looked at concrete football clubs. We tried to interpret their financial statistics using background knowledge. Last but not least, we provided a correlation analysis between financial statistics and sporting success.

There could be lots of ways to continue this research. One could go deeper into building a model for squad evaluation instead of relying on outside information. Furthermore, an analysis could also use a new metric whatsoever that will both look at the revenue and squad value of the team. Also, correlation analysis could be improved to interpret hypothesis testing.
